Core Operations · Findings & Recommendations

Five gaps holding back core operations — and how to close them.

Pay10's core operations runs on 6 operations specialists reporting up to Vishal and Ravi, and onward to Mohit. We sat with Vishal, Pooja and Shubham to map the day-to-day. The team spends its day on three repeating tasks — and our review surfaced five structural gaps, each with a clear, low-friction fix.

The Findings

What's happening today, and what we recommend instead.

01
/ 05
Daily task · MonitoringManual · 24×7

The dashboard is watched by eye, around the clock.

Specialists monitor the transaction dashboard 24×7 with the naked eye, manually refreshing the browser to catch a dip. Detection depends entirely on someone looking at the right moment — slow, fatiguing, and easy to miss during off-hours.

→ Recommendation — Automated SR alerting
  • Set threshold-based alerts on Success Rate (SR): the moment SR drops below a defined floor, the system fires automatically — no human polling required.
  • Route alerts to the right people instantly — Operations, the Tech team, and merchant SPOCs — so the right owner is engaged within seconds, not after a manual catch.
  • Free the team from staring at a screen; let them act on signals instead of hunting for them.
02
/ 05
Daily task · ReportingHourly · Manual

Hourly reports are generated and pasted by hand.

Every hour, specialists manually generate a report, post it to the WhatsApp group, and share it with merchants on request. For internal tracking these reports are largely redundant — repetitive effort that produces no new decision.

→ Recommendation — On-demand & scheduled report automation
  • Make internal reports request-driven from an internal chat — an automation tool fetches the numbers and returns them over WhatsApp or any chosen channel, on demand.
  • For merchants who need hourly reports, set a fixed automated schedule that delivers to them directly — no operator in the loop.
  • Eliminate the redundant manual cadence and reclaim operator hours for higher-value work.
03
/ 05
Daily task · Merchant supportUntracked

Merchant queries are solved — but never tracked.

Queries get resolved, yet nothing is recorded. We have no visibility into the common issues merchants face, no way to spot when a systemic fix would cut volume at the source, and no accountability on how fast we respond or resolve.

→ Recommendation — A proper ITSM practice
  • Stand up full ITSM: Service Requests, Incident Management, Change Management, and Problem Management — so recurring pain points roll up into systemic fixes.
  • Define SLAs for response and resolution time per issue and issue type, with structured ticket management end-to-end.
  • Mandate that KAMs log every merchant query — however small — into the system, turning scattered conversations into trend data and root-cause signals.
04
/ 05
Reputation · ExternalBlind spot

Public complaints surface on social media — unseen.

Operations has no visibility into who is raising a ticket or complaining about Pay10 on social platforms. These posts are public, they spread, and left unaddressed they materially damage Pay10's reputation — especially when our brand is named directly.

Live example · X (Twitter)

A user publicly tags a government handle over an 18-day pending refund paid through "gateway pay10". This is exactly the kind of mention that today reaches no one on the operations side — and shapes public perception unchallenged.

→ Recommendation — Social listening & response
  • Deploy social media monitoring for mentions of the Pay10 platform across channels — surfacing complaints the moment they appear.
  • Route flagged mentions into the same ITSM ticketing flow so each public issue is owned, tracked, and resolved — not just noticed.
  • Respond publicly and promptly to protect brand reputation and demonstrate accountability.
05
/ 05
Security · InternalData exposure

Personal WhatsApp is the enterprise comms channel.

Operations runs on personal WhatsApp numbers — not acceptable for an enterprise serving some of the country's largest businesses. Sensitive files move through personal, unmanaged accounts with no control over where they end up.

→ Recommendation — Secure, governed communication
  • Move all internal communication to a single, secure, company-controlled platform — no personal numbers in the workflow.
  • Enforce data-loss controls: no file leaves the system, and any external share requires explicit approval.
  • Lock down endpoints — block social media on office laptops to close avoidable security and data-leak vectors.

From reactive watching to a measured, accountable operation.

Four of these five gaps are solved by the same shift: replace manual, untracked effort with automation and a system of record. The fifth — secure communication — is the foundation the rest sits on. Together they cut operator load, protect the brand, and give leadership real numbers to manage by.
